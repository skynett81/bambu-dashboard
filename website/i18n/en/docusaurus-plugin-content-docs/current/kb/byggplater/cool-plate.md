# Cool Plate (Smooth PEI)

The Cool Plate is a smooth PEI plate that gives a glossy and even bottom surface on prints. It is included as the standard plate with most Bambu Lab printers.

## Properties

- **Surface:** Smooth, glossy bottom surface on prints
- **Material:** PEI (Polyetherimide) coating on a spring steel plate
- **Max bed temperature:** 90 °C

## Best suited for

- PLA (all variants) — without glue stick
- PLA+ — without glue stick
- PLA Silk — without glue stick
- PLA-CF — without glue stick

## Use with caution

:::danger PETG — always use glue stick
PETG adheres **extremely strongly** to smooth PEI. Without glue stick, you risk tearing off the PEI coating from the plate when removing the print. Always use glue stick as a barrier, or choose the Engineering Plate.
:::

## Maintenance

### After each print
- Let the plate cool to room temperature (40 °C or lower) before removing the print
- PLA releases from the plate on its own at 25–30 °C
- Flex the plate gently to release stubborn prints

### Weekly cleaning
Wipe the plate with isopropanol (IPA, 70–99%):
```
1. Apply IPA to a clean, lint-free paper towel (don't spray directly on plate)
2. Wipe the surface in one direction
3. Let dry completely (30 seconds) before the next print
```

### For poor adhesion
1. Wash with warm water and mild dish soap
2. Rinse thoroughly
3. Dry with lint-free paper
4. Finish with an IPA wipe

:::warning Do not use acetone
Acetone dissolves the PEI coating and permanently damages the plate. Only use IPA or water/soap.
:::

## Replacing the plate

The plate is worn when:
- The PEI coating is visibly damaged (scratches, scoring, flaking)
- Prints no longer adhere even after thorough cleaning
- The surface is uneven and gives a poor first layer

A Cool Plate typically lasts 200–500 prints with normal use and good maintenance.
